# 01 - Product Vision and Scope

## High Concept

Conquer Others is a third-person medieval action-combat prototype inspired by
Mount & Blade. The player personally fights while allied and enemy soldiers
engage in a small arena battle.

## MVP Goal

Validate that:

- Third-person medieval movement is readable and responsive.
- Directional melee combat rewards timing and correct defense.
- Simple team AI creates a functional small-group battle.
- The battle has a clear beginning, outcome, and restart flow.
- The core battle concept is promising enough to continue.

## Target

- Platform: Windows PC.
- Engine: Unity 6.3 LTS.
- Input: keyboard and mouse.
- Audience: players interested in medieval combat and tactical action games.

## Core Fantasy

The player is a captain who roams a hostile overworld as a free company,
hunting bandits and raiding enemy-held holds to survive and grow a warband. The
captain personally fights each battle through movement, directional attacks, and
directional blocks. This is about survival and growth, not conquest.

## Current Scope

- A free-roam overworld with click-to-move party marker, a day clock, roaming
  bandit parties, settlement assaults, gold, escalating threats, and a persistent
  tier-by-archetype warband.
- A party-survival economy: daily owned-land income against daily troop wages,
  morale (with desertion when it craters), and a Renown-driven leadership cap that
  must be earned to grow the warband.
- Troop progression — soldiers bank battle experience and are promoted up the
  tiers — and typed settlement recruitment (village/town/castle volunteer pools).
- Four runtime-generated regional battlefields selected by encounter.
- The player starts alone owning no hold, with a small starting warband (3
  militia); the first fight is whatever they march into — an enemy hold's
  garrison (assault) or a roaming bandit party (field battle).
- Sword-and-shield directional melee combat.
- Health, stamina, hit reactions, death, victory, defeat, and restart.
- Close third-person combat camera.
- Runtime-generated low-poly visuals and procedural audio.

## Success Criteria

- The game launches directly into a complete playable battle flow.
- One-on-one combat is understandable.
- The player can identify attack and block direction.
- A battle with eight fighters remains functional.
- Victory, defeat, and restart work reliably.
